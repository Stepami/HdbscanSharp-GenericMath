/u/irrenhouse on Why Are So Many Teams Playing A Back Three? [Analysis]
Matching your opponent is purely an exercise in nullifying them by man marking them as opposed to seeking to beat them. It is a negative approach, one both the players and manager fully understand when they go into the game. It is intended as an attempt to avoid defeat at all costs. Aside from set pieces, the team will be unlikely to lose or concede as long as they don't make errors. The teams that got smashed when they matched Chelsea, as Everton comes to mind was largely down to the fact that they were grossly incompetent at actually defending or actually marking their opponents man for man. As such they found themselves in a tactically unfamiliar formation, leaving their counterparts in often too much space.
